### **How to Fix a Refrigerator and Freezer That’s Too Warm**  

#### **1. Check the Cold Control or Temperature Control**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the refrigerator.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the control behind the temperature adjustment knob (usually inside the fridge).  
- **Step 3:** Remove the control and set it to the warmest setting.  
- **Step 4:** Use a multimeter (set to Rx1) to test for continuity by touching the probes to the terminals.  
- **Step 5:** If there’s no continuity, replace the control.  

#### **2. Test the Evaporator Fan Motor**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the fridge.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the fan in the freezer (usually behind a panel).  
- **Step 3:** Check if the fan spins freely by hand.  
- **Step 4:** Use a multimeter to test for continuity.  
- **Step 5:** If the fan is stuck or has no continuity, replace it.  

#### **3. Inspect the Defrost System (Timer, Thermostat, Heater)**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the fridge.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the defrost timer (often behind the fridge or inside the control panel).  
- **Step 3:** Manually advance the timer to see if the fridge cools. If not, replace it.  
- **Step 4:** Test the defrost thermostat for continuity (should have continuity when cold).  
- **Step 5:** Check the defrost heater for continuity—if it’s open, replace it.  

#### **4. Check the Condenser Fan Motor**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the fridge.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the fan near the compressor (rear or bottom of the fridge).  
- **Step 3:** Spin the fan by hand—if it’s stuck or noisy, replace it.  
- **Step 4:** Test for continuity with a multimeter.  

#### **5. Test the Temperature Sensor or Thermistor**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the fridge.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the sensor (usually inside the fridge or freezer).  
- **Step 3:** Measure its resistance with a multimeter—compare to manufacturer specs.  
- **Step 4:** If resistance is out of range, replace the sensor.  

#### **6. Inspect the Electronic Control Board**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the fridge.  
- **Step 2:** Check for visible burns or damage on the board.  
- **Step 3:** If other components test fine but the fridge still doesn’t cool, replace the board.  

**Note:** Always unplug the refrigerator before testing or replacing parts. If unsure, consult a professional.
