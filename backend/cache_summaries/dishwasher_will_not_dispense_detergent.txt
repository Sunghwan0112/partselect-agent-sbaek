### **How to Fix a Dishwasher That Won’t Dispense Detergent**  

#### **1. Check the Detergent & Rinse Aid Dispensers**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the dishwasher or turn off power at the breaker.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the detergent dispenser (usually inside the door).  
- **Step 3:** Inspect for cracks, discoloration, or damage.  
- **Step 4:** Clean any caked-on detergent or debris with a damp cloth.  
- **Step 5:** If damaged, replace the dispenser.  

#### **2. Inspect the Rinse Aid Cap**  
- **Step 1:** Open the rinse aid compartment (typically near the detergent dispenser).  
- **Step 2:** Check if the cap is loose, warped, or cracked.  
- **Step 3:** Tighten or replace the cap if damaged.  

#### **3. Examine the Door Spring & Hinge Pin**  
- **Step 1:** Ensure the door closes properly.  
- **Step 2:** Check for broken springs or misaligned hinge pins.  
- **Step 3:** Replace damaged parts if the door doesn’t seal tightly.  

#### **4. Test the Wax Motor (If Applicable)**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the dishwasher.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the wax motor (often behind the dispenser).  
- **Step 3:** Check for continuity with a multimeter.  
- **Step 4:** Replace if faulty.  

#### **5. Check the Bi-Metal Release (Older Models)**  
- **Step 1:** Remove the dispenser assembly.  
- **Step 2:** Inspect the bi-metal release for damage or corrosion.  
- **Step 3:** Replace if it doesn’t open during the wash cycle.  

#### **6. Test the Timer (Mechanical Models)**  
- **Step 1:** Manually advance the timer to the wash cycle.  
- **Step 2:** Listen for a click indicating the detergent release.  
- **Step 3:** If no click, replace the timer.  

**Need parts?** Check the manufacturer’s guide for replacements.  
**Still not working?** Contact a professional technician.
