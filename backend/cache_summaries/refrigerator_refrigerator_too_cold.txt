### **How to Fix a Refrigerator That’s Too Cold**  

#### **1. Check the Air Inlet Damper or Baffle**  
- **Unplug the refrigerator** for safety.  
- Locate the **damper or baffle** (where cold air enters the fridge).  
- Inspect for **damage or misalignment**.  
- Ensure the **control knob moves freely** and the linkage isn’t broken.  
- If damaged, **replace the damper or baffle**.  

#### **2. Test the Temperature Sensor or Thermistor**  
- Unplug the fridge.  
- Locate the **temperature sensor** (usually near the damper or inside the fridge).  
- Use a **multimeter** to check for proper resistance (refer to your fridge’s manual for specs).  
- If faulty, **replace the sensor**.  

#### **3. Inspect the Main Control Board**  
- Unplug the refrigerator.  
- Locate the **control board** (often behind the fridge or inside the control panel).  
- Check for **burn marks, loose wires, or damaged components**.  
- If defective, **replace the control board**.  

#### **4. Adjust or Replace the Cold Control/Temperature Control**  
- Unplug the fridge.  
- Locate the **temperature control knob or digital panel**.  
- Test the control with a multimeter (if applicable).  
- If the control is faulty or stuck, **replace it**.  

**Note:** If unsure about any step, consult a professional technician.
