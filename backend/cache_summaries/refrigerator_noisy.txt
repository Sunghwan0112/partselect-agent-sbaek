### **How to Fix a Noisy Refrigerator**  

#### **1. Check the Condenser Fan Motor**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the refrigerator.  
- **Step 2:** Remove the rear access panel (usually at the bottom back of the fridge).  
- **Step 3:** Locate the condenser fan (near the compressor).  
- **Step 4:** Inspect for debris, obstructions, or damage.  
- **Step 5:** If the fan is stuck or worn, replace the **condenser fan motor**.  

#### **2. Inspect the Evaporator Fan Motor**  
- **Step 1:** Unplug the fridge.  
- **Step 2:** Locate the evaporator fan (inside the freezer compartment, behind a panel).  
- **Step 3:** Check for ice buildup, debris, or a damaged fan blade.  
- **Step 4:** If the motor is noisy or seized, replace the **evaporator fan motor**.  

#### **3. Replace Worn Fan Grommets**  
- If the evaporator fan is vibrating loudly, the rubber **grommets** (mounting dampeners) may be worn.  
- Remove the old grommets and install new ones to reduce noise.  

#### **4. Additional Tips**  
- Clean dust from condenser coils (can cause overheating and noise).  
- Ensure the fridge is level (adjust the front leveling legs if needed).  

If the noise persists after these checks, further diagnosis may be needed.
